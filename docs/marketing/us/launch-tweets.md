## Article 1: What Is Carror OS

### Version A

Carror OS is not an IDE plugin. Not a prompt template. Not an agent framework.

It's a **governance layer** that runs underneath your AI tools:

- No evidence? Can't mark complete.
- Reaching for .env? System blocks it.
- Forgetting rules? System re-injects them.
- Starting a new session? Past errors are loaded.

A prompt is a suggestion. A gate is the power to allow or deny.

**Carror OS doesn't remind AI to do right. It decides whether AI can continue before it does wrong.**

**A reminder is not a constraint.**

By round 20, AI forgets, skips, guesses with confidence.

Carror OS doesn't hope AI remembers. It installs **mechanical gates** at the 5 most dangerous failure points.

## Article 3: 5 Gates

### Version A

5 gates that actually stop AI mistakes:

① **Completion gate** — No evidence, no "done"
② **Rule anchor** — Rules re-injected before every write
③ **Privacy gate** — Secrets blocked at system level
④ **Permission gate** — Dangerous commands require your confirmation
⑤ **Memory gate** — Past errors injected into new sessions

A prompt is a suggestion. A gate is the power to allow or deny.

### Version B

5 things Carror OS doesn't allow AI to do:

Can't claim completion without proof.
Can't forget rules (the system won't let it).
Can't read secrets.
Can't approve its own dangerous commands.
Can't make the same mistake a fifth time.

**Every rule is a mechanical constraint, not a suggestion.**
