# A Cup of Coffee

Worth it.

But I don't want to just say "worth it."

***

## Let's Do the Math First

A cup of coffee: around $5.

***

**Scenario One: One False Completion**

AI says it's done. You trust it. Ship it.

The other side finds the problem. You redo the work.

Conservative estimate: 2 hours.

Even at minimum wage, 2 hours is worth way more than $5.

`completion-gate` stops this once. Coffee money back.

***

**Scenario Two: One Secret Leak**

API Key enters the conversation context.

Best case: nothing happens, but you spend hours worrying, investigating, deciding whether to rotate keys.

Worst case: service abused, bill anomaly, emergency response.

`privacy-gate` stops this once. Coffee money doesn't even begin to describe its value.

***

**Scenario Three: Daily Mental Burden Saved**

There's no way to put a dollar amount on this.

But you know the feeling —

Late at night, staring at AI output, uncertain if it really finished, uncertain if it remembered the rules, uncertain if that assertion has a source.

This constant background anxiety eats away at you every day.

It doesn't consume your time. It consumes **the energy you need for the work that actually matters**.

Carror OS lifts this anxiety off your shoulders.

Every day.

***

## But What I Really Want to Say

Whether a cup of coffee is worth it is a question of consumer choice.

Behind that question is a bigger one:

**Are you willing to give a small nod of recognition to someone who worked alone, built guardrails from his own painful experiences, and gave them to you for free?**

Not paying for a product.

It's **a response**.

***

He spent three months turning every place AI had hurt him into your guardrails.

He didn't charge.

He said "it's not good enough yet."

He said "my personal energy and AI reliability are both limited, please don't be harsh."

The thing he most wanted to say in his marketing materials wasn't "how great this tool is." It was **"It treats the person I used to be. Also the person you are now."**

***

A cup of coffee isn't buying a tool.

It's saying:

**"I see what you did. Thank you."**

***

## One Last Sentence

A free, open-source tool. The author has no obligation to make it perfect, no obligation to maintain it indefinitely, no obligation to answer every question.

But he's doing all that.

Because he cares about the person working alone, burned by AI, who didn't stop.

If you're that person —

**A cup of coffee is the lightest response you can give, and the most genuine recognition he deserves.**

Worth it.

Of course it's worth it.
